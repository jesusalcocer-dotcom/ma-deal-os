# TERM SHEET — PROJECT MERCURY
## Proposed Acquisition of SpectraTech Solutions, Inc.

**CONFIDENTIAL — FOR DISCUSSION PURPOSES ONLY**
**Date: January 15, 2026**

---

### PARTIES

**Buyer:** Meridian Capital Partners VII, L.P., a Delaware limited partnership ("Buyer"), acting through a newly formed acquisition vehicle, Mercury Acquisition Corp., a Delaware corporation ("Acquisition Sub")

**Seller:** The stockholders of SpectraTech Solutions, Inc. (collectively, "Sellers"), including:
- TechVentures Fund III, LLC (42% holder)
- SpectraTech Management Holdings, LLC (28% holder)  
- Various individual stockholders (30% holders)

**Target:** SpectraTech Solutions, Inc., a Delaware corporation (the "Company")

### TRANSACTION STRUCTURE

Stock Purchase. Buyer (through Acquisition Sub) will purchase 100% of the outstanding shares of common stock of the Company from the Sellers.

### PURCHASE PRICE

**Base Purchase Price:** $185,000,000 (One Hundred Eighty-Five Million Dollars), subject to customary adjustments.

**Consideration:** All cash at closing, subject to the adjustments described below.

**Purchase Price Adjustments:**
- Working Capital Adjustment: Target working capital of $12.5 million (the "Target Working Capital"), with a true-up mechanism within 90 days post-closing based on final closing balance sheet. Collar of +/- $500,000 within which no adjustment applies.
- Net Debt Adjustment: Reduction for any indebtedness of the Company outstanding at closing.
- Transaction Expenses: Reduction for any unpaid transaction expenses of the Company and the Sellers at closing.

**Earnout:** Up to $25,000,000 in additional consideration payable in two tranches:
- Tranche 1: $15,000,000 if 2026 EBITDA exceeds $32,000,000
- Tranche 2: $10,000,000 if 2027 EBITDA exceeds $38,000,000
EBITDA to be calculated consistent with the Company's historical accounting practices, with customary add-backs.

### ESCROW AND HOLDBACK

**Indemnification Escrow:** 10% of the Base Purchase Price ($18,500,000) deposited into escrow at closing. 50% released on the 12-month anniversary of closing (subject to pending claims), remainder released on 18-month anniversary.

**Working Capital Holdback:** $2,500,000 held back pending final working capital determination.

### INDEMNIFICATION

**Approach:** Traditional indemnification with R&W insurance supplement.

**R&W Insurance:** Buyer to obtain a representations and warranties insurance policy. Buyer pays all premium and related costs (estimated at $1.2M). Policy limit of $18,500,000.

**Basket:** Tipping basket of $925,000 (0.5% of Base Purchase Price). Once the basket is exceeded, Sellers are liable from dollar one.

**Cap:** General cap of $18,500,000 (10% of Base Purchase Price), exclusive of Fundamental Representations and fraud.

**Fundamental Representations Cap:** Full Purchase Price.

**Survival:** General representations survive for 18 months post-closing. Fundamental representations (organization, authority, capitalization, brokers) survive for 36 months. Tax representations survive until 60 days after expiration of applicable statute of limitations.

### REGULATORY

**HSR Filing:** Required. Buyer and the Company to file within 10 business days after signing. Parties to use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain clearance.

**No CFIUS filing anticipated.**

**No industry-specific regulatory approvals required.**

### FINANCING

**Financing:** Buyer will fund the Purchase Price through a combination of:
- Equity commitment from Meridian Capital Partners VII, L.P. ($110,000,000)
- Senior secured term loan facility ($75,000,000) — commitment letter from First National Bank

**Financing Condition:** None. Buyer's obligation to close is not conditioned on obtaining financing.

### KEY EMPLOYEES

**Management Rollover:** CEO (James Morrison) and CTO (Sarah Chen) will each roll over 20% of their respective proceeds into equity of Acquisition Sub, in exchange for units of Buyer's co-invest vehicle.

**Employment Agreements:** New employment agreements with CEO, CTO, and VP Sales (David Park) effective at closing. Terms: 3-year initial term, at-will thereafter, subject to severance provisions.

**Non-Competes:** CEO, CTO, VP Sales, and VP Engineering (Lisa Tran) to execute 2-year post-closing non-competition agreements with geographic scope covering the United States and Canada.

### TRANSITION SERVICES

**TSA Required:** Yes. Seller (TechVentures) currently provides certain back-office services (accounting, payroll, benefits administration) to the Company through a shared services arrangement.

**Direction:** Seller to Buyer

**Term:** Up to 12 months post-closing, with option to extend for an additional 6 months on a service-by-service basis at cost plus 5%.

### CLOSING CONDITIONS

**Mutual Conditions:**
- HSR clearance (or expiration of waiting period)
- No material adverse effect
- Accuracy of representations (subject to MAE qualifier)
- Compliance with covenants in all material respects
- No injunctions or restraints

**Buyer Conditions:**
- Key Employee employment agreements and non-competes executed
- Rollover agreements executed
- Third-party consents for material contracts (Schedule to be agreed)

**Seller Conditions:**
- Payment of Base Purchase Price (less adjustments and escrow)

### GOVERNING LAW

Delaware

### EXCLUSIVITY

Sellers grant Buyer an exclusivity period of 60 days from execution of this term sheet (the "Exclusivity Period"), during which Sellers will not, directly or indirectly, solicit, encourage, or engage in discussions with any third party regarding a sale of the Company.

### NON-BINDING

This term sheet is non-binding except for the provisions regarding exclusivity, confidentiality, and governing law. This term sheet is intended to serve as the basis for negotiation of definitive documentation and does not constitute a commitment by either party.
