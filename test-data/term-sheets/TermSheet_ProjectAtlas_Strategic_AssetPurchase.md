# TERM SHEET — PROJECT ATLAS
## Proposed Acquisition of the Enterprise Software Division of Pinnacle Global Technologies, Inc.

**CONFIDENTIAL — FOR DISCUSSION PURPOSES ONLY**
**Date: January 22, 2026**

---

### PARTIES

**Buyer:** NovaStar Systems Corporation, a California corporation ("Buyer")

**Seller:** Pinnacle Global Technologies, Inc., a Delaware corporation ("Seller")

**Target Business:** The Enterprise Software Division of Seller (the "Business"), including all assets, intellectual property, customer contracts, and employees primarily related to the Business, as more particularly described herein.

### TRANSACTION STRUCTURE

Asset Purchase. Buyer will acquire substantially all of the assets used in or related to the Business, and assume certain specified liabilities, pursuant to an Asset Purchase Agreement.

This is a carveout transaction. The Business currently operates as a division of Seller and does not constitute a separate legal entity.

### PURCHASE PRICE

**Base Purchase Price:** $67,500,000 (Sixty-Seven Million Five Hundred Thousand Dollars)

**Consideration:**
- Cash at closing: $52,500,000
- Seller Note: $15,000,000 — subordinated promissory note bearing interest at 6.5% per annum, payable in equal quarterly installments over 3 years, with acceleration upon change of control of Buyer.

**Purchase Price Adjustments:**
- Working Capital Adjustment: Target working capital of $4.2 million attributable to the Business. True-up within 120 days post-closing based on agreed-upon methodology for allocating shared working capital items.

**No earnout.**

### ESCROW AND HOLDBACK

**Indemnification Escrow:** 8% of the cash portion of the Base Purchase Price ($4,200,000) deposited into escrow at closing. Released on the 15-month anniversary of closing (subject to pending claims).

**No working capital holdback** — adjustment to be settled by wire transfer.

### INDEMNIFICATION

**Approach:** Traditional indemnification (no R&W insurance).

**Basket:** True deductible of $675,000 (1% of Base Purchase Price). Sellers only liable for amounts in excess of the basket.

**Cap:** $10,125,000 (15% of Base Purchase Price) for general representations.

**Fundamental Representations Cap:** Full Purchase Price.

**Survival:** General representations survive for 15 months. Fundamental representations survive for 24 months. Environmental representations survive for 36 months. Tax representations survive for the applicable statute of limitations plus 90 days.

### REGULATORY

**No HSR Filing required** (transaction value below $119.5M threshold and no other basis for filing).

**No CFIUS concerns.**

**No industry-specific regulatory approvals required.**

### FINANCING

**Financing:** Buyer will fund the cash portion of the Purchase Price from cash on hand and an existing revolving credit facility.

**No financing condition.**

### KEY EMPLOYEES

**Employee Transfers:** Buyer to extend offers of employment to all employees primarily dedicated to the Business (approximately 85 employees). Seller to provide reasonable cooperation in employee transition.

**Key Employee Retention:** Buyer to offer retention bonuses to VP Product (Angela Martinez), Director of Engineering (Robert Kim), and Head of Customer Success (Jennifer Walsh) — aggregate retention pool of $1,200,000, payable 50% at closing and 50% on 12-month anniversary.

**Non-Competes:** Seller to execute a 3-year non-competition agreement restricting Seller from competing in the enterprise resource planning software market in North America. Key employees: 2-year non-competes for VP Product and Director of Engineering.

### TRANSITION SERVICES

**TSA Required:** Yes. Given the carveout nature of the transaction, Seller will provide the following transition services to Buyer:

**Direction:** Seller to Buyer

**Services:**
- IT infrastructure hosting and migration support (up to 9 months)
- Finance and accounting support, including payroll processing (up to 6 months)
- Facilities/real estate: sublease of current office space in Austin, TX (up to 12 months)
- HR administration and benefits transition (up to 6 months)
- Shared customer support platform access (up to 6 months)

**Pricing:** At cost for first 6 months, cost plus 10% thereafter.

### INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**Assigned IP:** All IP primarily used in or developed for the Business, including:
- Enterprise software platform (source code, object code, documentation)
- All registered trademarks related to the Business product lines
- All patents and patent applications primarily related to the Business
- All customer data related to the Business

**Licensed IP:** Seller will grant Buyer a perpetual, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use certain shared technology platforms and libraries used by both the Business and Seller's retained operations.

**Reverse License:** Buyer will grant Seller a 3-year transitional license to use certain Business IP that Seller's retained operations currently rely upon, at no cost.

### CLOSING CONDITIONS

**Mutual Conditions:**
- No material adverse effect on the Business
- Accuracy of representations (subject to MAE qualifier for Seller's reps; bring-down for Buyer's reps)
- Compliance with covenants
- No injunctions

**Buyer Conditions:**
- Third-party consents for material contracts (all customer contracts with ARR > $500K)
- Key employee acceptance of employment offers and non-competes
- Completion of intellectual property assignment documentation
- Delivery of audited carveout financial statements for the Business (FY 2024 and FY 2025)

**Seller Conditions:**
- Payment of cash portion and delivery of Seller Note
- Execution of TSA
- Execution of IP license agreements

### GOVERNING LAW

New York

### EXCLUSIVITY

45 days from execution of this term sheet.

### NON-BINDING

This term sheet is non-binding except for exclusivity and confidentiality.
